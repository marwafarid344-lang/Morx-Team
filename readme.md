## 🚀 Getting Started

Welcome to **Morx**! If you're reading this, you probably realized that a group chat named "Project Final Final_v3" isn't a sustainable way to manage tasks. Fear not!

### What is Morx?

Morx is what happens when productivity meets sanity. It's designed to help you:
- 👥 Build teams without the usual "who is doing what?" drama
- 📂 Organize projects so you don't lose that one important file ever again
- ✅ Actually track tasks instead of just hoping they get done by magic
- 🤝 Collaborate with humans seamlessly
- 📊 Judge—we mean, *analyze*—performance with pretty charts

---

## 👤 Creating Your Account (Joining the Cool Club)

### Sign Up

1. Smash that **"Sign Up"** button on the homepage.
2. Fill in your details:
   - First & Last Name
   - Email Address (preferably not the one you made in 5th grade)
   - Password (make it stronger than your morning coffee ☕)
3. Click **"Create Account"**. Enter the matrix. 🎉

### Complete Your Profile

Look, an empty profile is suspicious. Help us help you:

1. Add a **profile picture** - prove you're not three raccoons in a trench coat.
2. Set your **location** - so we know if you're answering messages at 3 AM.
3. Add your **skills** - show off a little!
4. Select your **department** (CS, AI, HI, DS, BA, or MA).
5. Set your **study level**.

### Signing In

1. Click **"Sign In"**.
2. Put in the details you just swore you'd remember.
3. Click **"Login"**. Welcome back to reality.

## 👥 Managing Your Teams (Herding the Cats)

### Creating a New Team

1. Click **"Teams"**.
2. Mash the **"Create Team"** button.
3. The wizard will guide you:

   **Step 1:** Give it a cool name. "The A-Team" is taken, sorry.

   **Step 2:** Is it a **Basic Team** (chill vibes) or a **Determinated Team** (serious academic business)?
   
   **Step 3:** (If Determinated) Why are we here? Project, Study, FCDS, or "Other"?
   **Step 4:** (If FCDS) Pick your academic poison (subject).

### Team Roles & Permissions

| Role | Power Level |
|------|-------------|
| **Owner** | The absolute boss. Can delete the team in a fit of rage. |
| **Admin** | The trusty sidekick. Can do almost everything but press the self-destruct button. |
| **Member** | The muscle. Does the actual work and leaves comments. |

### Inviting Members

1. Go to your team.
2. Click **"Invite Members"**.
3. Hunt down users or type their email.
4. Send the invite and wait patiently for them to leave you on read.

### Browsing Public Teams

1. Go to **"Teams"** → **"Browse Public"**.
2. Beg to join existing teams by clicking **"Request to Join"**.
3. Wait for the owner to deem you worthy.

### Leaving or Deleting a Team

**To Leave:**
- Click "Leave Team". See ya!

**To Delete (Owners only):**
- Click the three dots → **"Delete Team"**.
- Type "delete my team" to prove you're not just wildly clicking around.
> ⚠️ **Warning**: Deleting a team nukes EVERYTHING permanently. There is no "undo" button. Be very, very sure.

## ✅ Task Management (Where Dreams Become Deadlines)

### Creating a Task

1. Click **"Add Task"** or the **"+"** button.
2. **Title**: What do we need to do?
3. **Description**: How do we do it? (Be nice to future-you).
4. **Priority**: High 🔴 (We're on fire), Medium 🟡 (Getting warm), Low 🟢 (Maybe next year).
5. **Due Date**: The day the panic sets in.
6. **Assignee**: Tag, you're it!

### Task Statuses

| Status | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| 📋 **To-Do** | Looking at this gives me anxiety. |
| 🔄 **In Progress** | I have 40 tabs open and one of them is this task. |
| ✅ **Done** | Time for a nap. |

**How to move 'em?** Just drag and drop like a boss.

### Adding Comments

1. Open a task.
2. Type your thoughts. Emojis encouraged.
3. Hit **"Post Comment"**.
4. Leave aggressive 👍s on your teammates' replies.

### Documentation (Markdown Magic)

Need to get technical? Use our Markdown editor!
- `#` for big loud headings
- `**text**` to **shout**
- `-` for satisfying lists
- Triple backticks for those sweet, sweet code snippets right out of StackOverflow.

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

1. **Don't make everything High Priority 🔴.** If everything is an emergency, nothing is.
2. **Actually mark tasks as Done ✅.** It releases dopamine, we promise.
3. **Use the description box.** "Do the thing" is not a valid task description, Kevin.
